Salem Witch Trials: Key Figures

Betty Parris

Betty Parris, a central figure in the Salem Witch Trials of 1692, exhibited mysterious symptoms, sparking the hysteria that led to numerous accusations and trials in Salem.

Abigail Williams

Abigail Williams, cousin to Betty Parris, played a crucial role in the witch trials by making accusations and fueling the fear that led to unjust trials and executions.

Tituba

Tituba, a slave in the Parris household, was one of the first accused of witchcraft. Her confession and accusations added fuel to the escalating hysteria in Salem.

Rebecca Nurse

Rebecca Nurse, a respected figure in the community, became a tragic victim of the trials. Falsely accused of witchcraft, she was wrongfully executed, highlighting the injustice of the era.

John Proctor

John Proctor, a farmer, who was accused of witchcraft. His adament refusal to confess and his eventual execution symbolized resistance to the hysteria.

Giles Corey

Giles Corey, an elderly man, was pressed to death with large stones for refusing to enter a plea. His tragic end became a symbol of resistance against the unjust legal proceedings during the trials.
